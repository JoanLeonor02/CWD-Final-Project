GEOGRAPHY

Laguna

A province in the Philippines situated in the CALABARZON region occupying the central section of Luzon. Its capital is the Municipality of Santa Cruz.

"City of Life"

Biñan City has been gifted with rich history and heritage that we still enjoy today. One of the few progressive places in the Philippines where one still sees a lot of age-old traditions being practiced and are very much a part of the day-to-day life of the people.

Discover More about Biñan City

"The Enterprise City of the Philippines"

Cabuyao City is known as “The Enterprise City of the Philippines,” where all business opportunities are everywhere. This city was also known before as one of the richest local government units in the country due to its large number of immigrants who are working.

Discover More about Cabuyao City

"Lion City of the South"

Santa Rosa City in Laguna is known as the "Lion City of the South" due to its rapid economic growth and development over the years which contributes to Santa Rosa's reputation as a strong, powerful, and thriving city in the south, akin to the strength and majesty of a lion. It is home to several major industrial parks and economic zones, making it a key manufacturing and business hub in the region.

Discover More about Santa Rosa City

Did you know?

Laguna covers a total area of 1,917.85 km2 (740.49 sq mi) occupying the north-central section of the Calabarzon region in Luzon. The province is situated southeast of Metro Manila, south of Rizal province, west of Quezon, north of Batangas and east of Cavite.

"City of Springs"

Calamba City is famous for its numerous hot springs, particularly in the area of Pansol, which have become popular destinations for both locals and tourists.

Discover More about Calamba City

"City of Seven Lakes"

San Pablo City is famous for its seven crater lakes which include Lake Sampaloc, Lake Bunot, Lake Palakpakin, Lake Calibato, Lake Mohicap, Lake Pandin, and Lake Yambo.

Discover More about San Pablo City

"Laguna's Gateway to Metro Manila"

San Pedro City is the boundary between Laguna and Metro Manila. It shares boundaries with Metro Manila southernmost city, Muntinlupa, Biñan, Dasmariñas, Carmona and Gen. Mariano Alvarez. Its position makes San Pedro a popular suburban residential community, where many residents commute daily to Metro Manila for work.

Discover More about San Pedro City

Did you know?

Laguna is the third largest province in the Calabarzon region and the 63rd largest in the entire country. The municipalities of Cavinti and the city of San Pablo have the largest land areas with 203.58 km2 (78.60 sq mi) and 197.56 km2 (76.28 sq mi), respectively. The municipality of Victoria has the smallest land area with 57.46 km2 (22.19 sq mi).

Kesong Puti

This fresh and soft cheese is crafted from whole carabao milk which is known for its rich, creamy flavor and smooth texture. It can be enjoyed on its own with bread, or used in various dishes such as pastas or burgers. Its flavor is delectable and satisfying, providing a richness that traditional yellow cheeses may lack.

Bibingka de Macapuno

A delightful rice cake that features a luscious filling of macapuno that is a tender processed coconut meat. This traditional Filipino treat combines the soft and moist texture of rice cake with sweetness of macapuno, creating a satisfying and flavorful experience and offers a unique and rich coconut flavor that balances its sweetness.

Tio Casio's Bibingka de Macapunoruz
favorite
Puto Biñan

Puto is grinded rice mixed with eggs and cheese. Puto Biñan makes all the difference to the common delicacy in the Philippines as it has many variations with different flavors and sizes making it unique to all other traditional putos out there. Some have fillings inside, on top, and can be as large as the size of a pizza.

Popular Shop:

Nila's Puto Biñan

favorite
Espasol

Espasol is a traditional Filipino delicacy that is known for its distinctive snowy appearance and unique flavor. It is a type of rice cake made from glutinous rice flour, which is toasted and then cooked in a mixture of coconut milk and sugar. The rice cake is then rolled and shaped into cylindrical pieces.


favorite
Kinulob na Itik

Kinulob na Itik is a unique and traditional Filipino dish that originates from Victoria, Laguna. It features duck that is cooked in a flavorful broth before being deep-fried which helps to infuse it with rich, savory flavors while also making the meat tender and juicy. It is then deep-fried to achieve a crispy, golden-brown skin, making it an enticing dish to those who enjoy fried poultry.

ATTRACTIONS

San Agustin Parish Church

Historically considered as the oldest Roman Catholic church in the Province of Laguna upon the early conquest and establishment of La Laguna.

Isdaan Floating Restaurant

Offers a unique dining experience to guests with its Thai-inspired motif and a generally festive atmosphere. It features bamboo cottages that are built on a pond, thus giving the illusion of floating native huts. Despite Isdaan's theme, however, the menu is predominantly composed of Filipino cuisine.

Saint James the Apostle Parish Church

Also known as The Roman Catholic Church of Saint James the Apostle or commonly known as Church of Paete, is the only Roman Catholic church in Paete, Laguna, Philippines. Its patron saint is James the Apostle and his feast is celebrated every 25 July.

Makiling Botanic Garden

Established on June 20, 1963 under Republic Act 3523, primarily to support professional instruction and research related to forestry and plant sciences and to serve the educational, recreational and tourism needs of the public. MBG is a special facility managed by the Makiling Center for Mountain Ecosystems of the UPLB College of Forestry and Natural Resources (CFNR).

Esmeris Farm

A quiet and serene place, perfect for people who just want to take a breather from their stressful lives and enjoy the simple beauty of nature. Before setting your feet on the farm, you will have to walk about 400 meters from the parking area. It's like you're hiking up the mountains because Esmeris farm is literally hidden from the busy town proper. While this might seem exhausting, there's no other feeling but satisfaction when you make it to the farm.

Pagsanjan Falls

One of the most popular tourist destinations in the Philippines. The town of Pagsanjan is known as the Tourist Capital of the province. The main attraction in the area is Pagsanjan Falls, which is considered one of the most well-known waterfalls in the country.
